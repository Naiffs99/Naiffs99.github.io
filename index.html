About

Strategist. Institution Builder. National Contributor.

I've spent the past 13 years at the intersection of strategy and execution — not as an outside adviser, but as the person building the systems from within. My career has centered on a single question: how do institutions translate national ambition into measurable results?

At Elm, Monsha'at, and now SAIP, I've built planning and performance functions from the ground up — designing the frameworks, leading the teams, and owning the outcomes at each stage of Saudi Arabia's transformation journey.

At SAIP, I lead the Strategy Management Office across the full enterprise: from formulating long-term strategy to overseeing execution through KPIs, dashboards, and portfolio management. Beyond the institution, I represent Saudi Arabia's intellectual property interests in international forums, including a founding role in the Saudi Fund-in-Trust initiative at WIPO.

I believe the best strategy work is invisible to the public — but it is foundational to everything that works.
